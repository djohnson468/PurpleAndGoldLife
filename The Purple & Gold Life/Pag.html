Brief History of Prairie view A&M University

  • Establishment: Created on August 14, 1876, as "Alta Vista Agricultural and Mechanical College of Texas for Colored Youth".
  • Origins: It was a direct result of the Texas Constitution of 1876, which called for separate, equal, and public education for Black and white children.
  • First Enrollment: The school opened in 1878 with eight young men, initially facing low patronage and financial struggle

Facts

  • "Prairie View Produces Productive People"
  • Engineering Excellence: Powerhouse for minority engineers
  • Research: One of the first Black public schools to lead innovation
  • First-ever Historically Black College and University (HBCU) to lead a national-tier University Transportation Center (UTC)

    Traditions

    • Panther Camp (Freshmen): A week long program introducing freshmen to campus history and spirit.
    • Class Shuffles & Dances: Fun student led traditions.
    • Homecoming: One of the biggest in the nation includes parades, tailgating, arts, food, and more.
    • Hump Day: This occurs every Wednesday (music, socializing, and Panther spirit).
