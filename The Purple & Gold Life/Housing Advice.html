Application Process

      You will need:
  • Classification
  • Current address
  • Contact information
  • Guarantor
  • Information about previous living situation
      The application will ask you where you want to stay; always have multiple alternatives in case your first choice isn't available. Housing is on a first come fisrt serve basis with renewals taking priority. The sooner you apply, the more likely you are to get your first choice. All leasings are independent. If accepted, an email will be sent, and you have 2-3 days to accept.

Pros of living on Campus:

  • Convenience
    • -You're close to classes, the library, and dining halls
  • More Involvement
    • -Clubs, study groups, and events
  • Safety & Support
    • -Campus security, Resident advisors

Cons of living on Campus:

  • Rules & Restrictions
    • -Guest Policies, Quiet hours
  • Meal plans
    • -Required dining plans may be costly
    -Doesn't fit your eating habits
  • Limited space
    • -Dorm rooms are often small with minimal storage

Application Process

      You will need:
  • Identification
  • Income Information
  • Employment Information
      The application process may vary by apartment. They may ask for your Social Security number to run a background check and/or a credit check. Off-campus housing can fill up quickly, so it is very important to apply as soon as you know where you want to stay and have the funds for the application fee. It is required to have renters' insurance; some places will offer their own, or you can purchase a plan separately. You must read your lease thoroughly and have a good understanding of all the rules and policies.

Pros of living off Campus

  • More Indepence
  • -You have greater control over your space, schedule, and lifestyle
  • Real-world experience
    • -Managing bills and leases builds life skills

Cons of living off campus

  • Lease Commitments
    • -Locked into a eyar long lease
  • More Responsbilty
    • -You handle maintenance requests, bills, and landlord communication

Lastly, there is Commuting:

Who's It For?

      Students who are committed to earning their degree, work off campus, or do not receive enough financial aid to cover on-campus housing may find commuting a more practical and affordable option. This option requires strong time management skills and reliable transportation.

Pros of Commuting

  • More Privacy
    • -No Roomates or dorm rules
  • Cost
    • -Potential to save money on housing and meal plans

Cons of Commuting

  • Time consuming
    • -Travel time can cut into studying, rest, and social life
  • Transportation Costs
    • -Gas, Parking permit, and Maintenance fees add up
