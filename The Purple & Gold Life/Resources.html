Meal Plans

How to apply for your meal plan:

  1. Go to PV Place on the pvamu website
  2. Click Panthertracks
  3. Click 'Choose a Meal Plan' under drop-menu 'terms'
  4. Click the current term under the drop menu 'terms'
  5. Select your meal plan
  6. Click Submit

Note: Meal Plans are available to select at the beginning of the semester. You have until the 5th day of class to select or change your meal plan. Wait 24 hours after selecting your meal plan for your funds to be activated.

For more information regarding meal plans visit: https://www.pvamu.edu/auxiliaryservices/food-services/meal-plan-options/

Determining Which Meal Plan is Best For You!

The meal plans are separated by dining hall meals and Pantherbucks (meal points).

Your dining hall meals are essentially the paid-for meals you get from dining in the cafeteria, the zone, and some food items in the Strike Zone. These meal swipes are limited per week and reset every Monday morning. If you go over your allotted amount of meal swipes for the week, you will have to use another form of payment.

Your Pantherbucks (meal points) are essentially money that you can spend outside of dining halls. These can be spent at all of the participating locations.

  • The Strike Zone (on campus)
  • Chick-fil-a (on campus)
  • The Bistro (on campus)
  • Panther Mart (on campus)
  • Dunkin' Donuts (on campus)
  • PV Mart (on campus)
  • Yang's Kitchen (off campus)
  • Waller County Line (off campus)

...and more!

For the fall and spring semesters, the meal plans are dispensed like so:

Meal Plan Points Fee
17 Meals 300 $1786.45 (Mandatory for Freshmen)
14 Meals 375 $1786.45
10 Meals 400 $1786.45
7 Meals 425 $1786.45 (Available for Seniors only)

Another thing to note: the more meal swipes you have, the less pantherbucks you have and vice versa. If you'd frequent the dining hall the most, select the meal plan with more meals. If you do not visit the dining hall often, select the meal plan with less meals and more points.

For more information regarding food services visit: https://www.pvamu.edu/auxiliaryservices/food-services/

Transportation

With the proximity of the campus, Prairie View provides free transportation for on-campus and off-campus students. These shuttles travel all over campus and even to nearby areas like the outlet mall and grocery stores.

The Black Route travels from the MSC, to Panther Crossing, The Gates, Panther Quarters, Fountain Hill, the Retail Center, The View, New Science Building, Hobart Taylor, and returns to the MSC.

The Gold Route travels from Panther Hill, Brookside University Trails, Creekside, the MSC, Phases 1&2, The Juvenile Justice Building, Phases 3&6, The Engineering Building, University College, the New Science Building, the library, Hobart Taylor, and returns to the MSC.

Both Routes run Monday through Friday from 7am to 9pm.

For more information regarding transportation visit: https://www.pvamu.edu/auxiliaryservices/transportation-services/

Study Areas

There are various areas across campus for students to find their ideal study space.

At the library, there are areas on the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd floors that you can utilize. For a more secluded study session, the 1st floor has a designated quiet area, there are study nooks available on the 2nd and 3rd floors. There are also study rooms that you can reserve. The library has many resources from computers to whiteboards, to make for the perfect study session.

In many of the on-campus housing there are study rooms available. These rooms can be accessed near your own dorm. Many of these rooms are available 24/7 and have whiteboards, TVs, and seclusion.

Many of your class schedule locations make for great study areas! Many of the buildings across campus have areas with chairs and tables that make for a great study session before and after class. Some common buildings students go to study are the Agriculture Business Building, the C. L. Wilson Engineering Building, and the new Engineering Building.
